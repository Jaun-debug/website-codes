Highlights to Visit

A curated selection of the most magnificent locations you will encounter on your self-drive adventure across Namibia.

Sossusvlei Dunes

Namibia's most famous highlight. Towering apricot-colored dunes rise dramatically against clear blue skies. A sunrise climb up Dune 45 or Big Daddy offers views that redefine the concept of vastness, while Deadvlei's ancient, stark acacia trees provide unparalleled photographic moments.

Etosha National Park

One of Africa's greatest wildlife sanctuaries. Park your 4x4 by a waterhole and let the safari come to you. From graceful antelope and towering giraffes to the elusive black rhino and prides of desert-adapted lions, Etosha offers profound, uninterrupted game viewing.

Fish River Canyon

As the second largest canyon in the world, the sheer scale of the Fish River Canyon is humbling. Standing on the rim at sunset, watching the plunging ravines change colors from amber to deep purple, is a truly spiritual experience for any self-driver passing through the deep south.

Sandwich Harbour

Located south of Walvis Bay, this is where the colossal, restless dunes of the Namib Desert cascade directly into the crashing waves of the Atlantic Ocean. It is a harsh, surreal, and astonishingly beautiful coastal phenomenon only accessible by expert 4x4 navigation.

Kalahari Desert

Far from barren, the Kalahari is a living desert of longitudinal red dunes covered in golden grasses. It pulses with life, from the majestic black-maned lions to foraging meerkats. It's an essential, deeply quiet stop offering profound starry nights.

Himba Culture

Venture into the remote Kunene region to interact with the semi-nomadic Himba people. Identifiable by the striking red otjize paste they use on their skin and hair, gaining insight into their enduring, resilient lifestyle is a deeply moving cultural experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I plan for my safari? +

For a comprehensive Namibia self-drive covering the major highlights (Sossusvlei, Swakopmund, Damaraland, and Etosha), we recommend a minimum of 12 to 14 days. If you wish to reach the far south (Fish River Canyon) or the Caprivi Strip, 18 to 21 days is ideal.

What are the main driving rules in national parks? +

In parks like Etosha, you must stay inside your vehicle at all times unless at a designated fenced rest camp. The speed limit inside the park is 60km/h to protect wildlife. Night driving is strictly prohibited; gates close at sunset and open at sunrise.

What happens if I get a flat tyre or a mechanical issue? +

All our vehicles come with two spare tyres and the necessary tools. We provide a full briefing on how to change a tyre before you depart. For mechanical issues, our 24/7 backup team is a phone call away and will arrange immediate repairs or a replacement vehicle.

Will I have mobile or GPS signal everywhere? +

Mobile reception is very good in towns, villages, and most lodges. However, while driving between destinations (especially in Damaraland or the deep south), you will lose signal. We provide detailed offline tracks and maps so you never get lost.
